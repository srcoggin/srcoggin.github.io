The Miami Dolphins have landed a defensive coordinator for new head coach Jeff Hafley, moving quickly to fill one of the most consequential jobs on an NFL staff as the franchise reshapes its identity on that side of the ball.

According to Pro Football Talk, the Dolphins are set to hire Sean Duggan as defensive coordinator. The move gives Hafley a top lieutenant tasked with translating the head coach’s vision into weekly game plans, play-calling decisions and a cohesive system — responsibilities that often determine whether a first-year coach can stabilize early or spends a season chasing consistency.

Duggan’s hiring is significant because the defensive coordinator role is typically the clearest on-field extension of the head coach. In Miami, that dynamic will be magnified under Hafley, whose early tenure will be evaluated not just by wins and losses but by whether the Dolphins’ defense develops a clear personality — disciplined in structure, reliable in situational football and capable of producing the kinds of stops that swing close games.

The Dolphins’ decision to identify a coordinator also provides clarity for the rest of the staff-building process. Once the defensive structure is in place, teams can more efficiently fill out position coaches and support roles, aligning teaching methods and terminology across the unit. That continuity matters in the offseason, when install periods are limited and every rep is designed to build familiarity before training camp.

For fans, the coordinator hire is also a tangible indicator of how Hafley intends to operate. Defensive play-calling is among the most scrutinized weekly responsibilities in the league, with decisions on pressure packages, coverage tendencies and red-zone approaches often becoming a referendum on a team’s preparation. Hiring a coordinator early in a head coach’s tenure can help set expectations for scheme and deployment, even if the full blueprint will not become clear until practices begin.

Pro Football Talk reported only that the Dolphins have found a defensive coordinator for Hafley and that Duggan will fill the post. Further details — including staff structure, terminology, and how the Dolphins will organize responsibilities between coaching and personnel — figure to come as the hire becomes official and the team continues rounding out Hafley’s first staff.
