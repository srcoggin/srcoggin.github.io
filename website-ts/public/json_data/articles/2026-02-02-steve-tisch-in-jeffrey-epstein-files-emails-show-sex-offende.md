Newly released emails tied to the Jeffrey Epstein files have drawn unwanted scrutiny to New York Giants co-owner Steve Tisch, a development that lands on the franchise at a time when front-office credibility and public trust matter as much as roster construction in the NFL’s free-agency season. CBS Sports NFL reported that the emails indicate Epstein — the late financier and convicted sex offender — scouted women for Tisch in 2013, an allegation likely to intensify questions around one of the league’s most visible ownership groups.

According to CBS Sports, the release includes emails that show Epstein acting as a go-between in identifying women for Tisch. The report characterizes the correspondence as evidence that Epstein “scouted women” for the Giants co-owner. The emails date to 2013, years after Epstein had already become a notorious figure and well before his death in 2019 while awaiting trial on federal sex trafficking charges.

The Giants have long operated with a relatively small, public-facing ownership structure compared with some NFL franchises.
