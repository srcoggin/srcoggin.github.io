The NFL said Monday it will look into newly released Justice Department emails that show convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein connected New York Giants co-owner Steve Tisch with numerous women, placing one of the league’s most prominent ownership figures under scrutiny at a time when the league has emphasized conduct and reputation as central to its business.

The league’s response came after the emails were released Friday, according to ESPN. The NFL said it is aware of the material and will review it, signaling that the matter has moved beyond an online controversy and into the realm of potential league action. While the NFL did not outline what steps it might take, the acknowledgment alone carries weight in a league where ownership stability and public trust are foundational to franchise value and league credibility.

Tisch is a co-owner of the Giants, one of the NFL’s flagship franchises, and a highly visible figure in the organization.
