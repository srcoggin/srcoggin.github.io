CBS Sports NFL reported that McVay and Snead have agreed to multi-year contract extensions, ensuring the coach-executive tandem remains in place as the Rams continue navigating the league’s annual free-agency churn and the longer-term project of sustaining success in a competitive NFC.

In an NFL where coaching changes and front-office shakeups often signal a reset, Los Angeles is opting for consistency.

The pair’s partnership has frequently been viewed as interconnected, with the head coach and general manager aligned in how they build the roster and set priorities. By extending both, the Rams avoid the uncertainty that can arise when a team’s top decision-makers approach the ends of their deals, particularly when that uncertainty can ripple into staffing, player evaluations and contract negotiations.

The timing also lands in the heart of the NFL’s roster-construction calendar, when teams are simultaneously evaluating their own talent and considering outside additions.
