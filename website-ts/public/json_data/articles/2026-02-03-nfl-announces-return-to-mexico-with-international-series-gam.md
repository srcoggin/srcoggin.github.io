The NFL is headed back to Mexico City, restoring one of the league’s most prominent international stages after a four-year absence and signaling a renewed push to expand its footprint beyond the United States. The league announced it will play an International Series game in Mexico City next season, beginning a three-year run that brings regular-season football back to one of the NFL’s most established overseas markets.

The return ends a hiatus that has kept Mexico City off the international calendar for four seasons. According to CBS Sports NFL, the league’s announcement confirms Mexico will again be part of the International Series, with a game scheduled for next season and additional games slated over the following two seasons. Specific teams, dates and venue details were not included in the source material.

Even without those particulars, the significance is clear: Mexico has long been viewed by the league as a cornerstone international market, and Mexico City has previously served as a showcase stop for regular-season games.
