Restricted free agency is designed to give clubs leverage in retaining younger players, but it also creates a window for aggressive teams to hunt for contributors who may be poised for a bigger role.

Elliss’ case underscores why the mechanism matters. Restricted free agents can negotiate with other teams, but the original team has matching rights on any offer sheet, and draft-pick compensation can come into play depending on the tender level. That structure often chills the market, and it forces interested teams to decide whether the potential upgrade is worth tying up cap space while waiting on a match — and possibly surrendering draft capital. The Yahoo Sports report said the Raiders were close to getting Elliss, indicating Las Vegas pushed far enough to make the move a real possibility before it fell short.
