The NFL said it “will look into” reported emails between New York Giants co-owner Steve Tisch and Jeffrey Epstein, a development that adds an unwelcome off-field storyline for one of the league’s flagship franchises and raises fresh questions about what, if anything, the league knew and when.

In a statement acknowledged by Yahoo Sports, the league confirmed it was aware of the emails, which Tisch also acknowledged. The NFL’s response — that it will review the matter — signals the issue has moved beyond a one-day flashpoint and into the league’s broader accountability framework, even if no disciplinary parameters have been outlined publicly and no timeline has been provided for what a review might entail.

The Giants are among the NFL’s most prominent organizations, playing in the country’s largest media market and carrying a long history of success. Their ownership group, which includes Tisch, is a visible part of the franchise’s public identity, making any scrutiny of leadership significant to fans who track not just wins and losses but the stewardship of a team that operates as both a business and a civic brand.

Epstein, a convicted sex offender who died in jail in 2019 while awaiting federal trial on sex trafficking charges, remains a figure whose associations and communications are closely examined. Any connection to Epstein — even one limited to emails — can prompt public and corporate fallout, particularly in the sports world, where league and team partners prioritize reputational risk. For the NFL, the matter intersects with the league’s emphasis on conduct and governance, an area it has increasingly highlighted amid heightened attention to leadership standards across professional sports.

Yahoo Sports reported the league acknowledged being aware of the emails. Tisch, according to the report, acknowledged the existence of the communications as well. The NFL did not provide further detail about what the emails contained, the dates involved or whether the league has requested documents from the Giants or Tisch. No additional statements from the Giants were included in the source material.

For Giants fans, the story matters because ownership stability and credibility can have downstream effects on how a franchise operates, from front-office leadership to the confidence of sponsors and the tone set at the top. Even without any immediate football-related penalties, the league’s decision to look into the matter introduces uncertainty at a time when franchises typically prefer to keep focus on roster building and on-field priorities.

What comes next will hinge on whether the NFL’s review remains preliminary or expands into a more formal process.
