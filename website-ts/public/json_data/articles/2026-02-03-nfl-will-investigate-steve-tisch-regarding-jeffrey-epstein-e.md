The NFL said it will investigate New York Giants co-owner Steve Tisch after emails linking Tisch to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein surfaced publicly, a development that adds an off-field cloud over one of the league’s most visible franchises and its ownership group. The league’s decision, announced in a brief statement, comes three days after the emails came to light and signals that the NFL is treating the matter as more than a passing headline.

Both Yahoo Sports and Pro Football Talk reported the league issued a two-sentence statement acknowledging it is looking into the situation involving Tisch. The statement did not include further details about what the investigation will examine, how long it is expected to take or whether the Giants or Tisch have been asked to provide information. The league also did not announce any interim measures while the review proceeds.

Tisch has long been one of the public faces of the Giants’ ownership, sharing control of the franchise with the Mara family.
