The Seattle Seahawks are expected to go up for sale after Super Bowl LX, a development that could reshape one of the NFL’s most recognizable franchises and add another marquee asset to a pro sports ownership market that has grown increasingly competitive. League and ownership sources familiar with the arrangement told ESPN that the team will be put on the market following the league’s championship game.

The timing is notable because Super Bowl LX is still ahead on the NFL calendar, giving the franchise, the league and potential bidders a defined window before a formal process begins. Any sale of an NFL club carries leaguewide significance, but the Seahawks represent a particularly high-profile opportunity given the team’s national brand, modern stadium situation and standing as the lone NFL franchise in the Pacific Northwest. For Seahawks fans, the news introduces a new layer of uncertainty and curiosity about what a future ownership group could mean for the team’s long-term direction.

ESPN reported that the plan is understood by league and ownership sources, an indication that the expected sale is not a sudden pivot but a timeline that has been communicated at high levels. The NFL’s ownership transfer process typically requires approval from the league’s finance committee and a vote by fellow owners, making these transactions more than private business deals. Even when a buyer is identified, the process can take months as the league reviews financing, governance structure and compliance with ownership policies.

For a franchise like Seattle, a sale can touch nearly every part of the organization beyond the front office: long-term stadium and facility planning, business operations, and the ability to invest aggressively in football infrastructure. It also matters competitively because stability at the ownership level often sets the tone for high-stakes decisions — including hiring and retaining top executives and coaches — even as roster building is governed by the salary cap and the draft.

The news also arrives in an era when NFL teams have become among the most valuable properties in North American sports, drawing interest from deep-pocketed individuals and investment groups. A Seahawks sale would likely attract wide attention across the sports world, not only for the size of the transaction but for what it signals about the league’s continued financial trajectory.

What comes next will be defined by timelines. The key checkpoint is the completion of Super Bowl LX, after which the franchise is expected to be placed on the market, according to ESPN.
